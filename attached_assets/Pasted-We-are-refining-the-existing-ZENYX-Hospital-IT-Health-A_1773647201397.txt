QUESTION STYLE RULES
- Avoid technical jargon in the main question
- Make questions understandable for owners, managers, doctors, and IT staff
- Keep helper text short and useful
- Questions should feel more human, engaging, and premium

EXAMPLE STYLE TO FOLLOW
Good:
“Do you get alerts when internet, firewall, or server issues happen?”
Helper:
“Early alerts help prevent downtime from affecting billing, records, and daily operations.”

UNIVERSAL QUESTIONS TO KEEP / IMPROVE
Use these improved plain-English questions:
- Do you have backup internet connectivity?
- How often does IT or network downtime affect daily work?
- Is Wi-Fi stable in important working areas?
- Do all computers have antivirus or endpoint security?
- Is there a firewall protecting your network?
- Is system access controlled by staff role or department?
- Is hospital data backed up automatically?
- Are backups tested to confirm recovery works?
- Do you get alerts when important systems go down?
- Do critical systems have UPS or generator backup?
